## Section: What Does Merging PDFs Mean?

```markdown
## What Does Merging PDFs Mean?

Merging (or combining) PDFs means taking two or more separate PDF files and joining them into a single PDF document. The pages appear in the order you choose, exactly as they were in the original files.

Common reasons to merge PDFs:

- **Work and business:** Combine a cover letter, resume, and portfolio into one file before emailing a job application
- **Finance and admin:** Merge monthly bank statements or invoices into a single annual document for tax records or bookkeeping
- **Education:** Combine lecture notes, handouts, and assignments into one document before printing or submitting
- **Legal documents:** Join a contract, appendices, and signature pages into one complete file
- **Projects:** Merge chapters, reports, or sections written separately into a final compiled document

Instead of asking a recipient to open five attachments, a merged PDF gives them everything in one place with the correct page order.
```

## Section: Tips for Better PDF Merging

```markdown
## Tips for Better Results

**Check page orientation before merging.** If some files are portrait and others landscape, the merged PDF will mix orientations. Rotate pages in the individual files first if needed.

**Compress large PDFs before merging.** If the combined file will be very large, use our [PDF Compressor](/tools/compress-pdf) on the individual files first, then merge. This keeps the final file manageable for email.

**Name your files clearly before uploading.** When you upload multiple files, having descriptive filenames (e.g. "01-cover-letter.pdf", "02-resume.pdf") makes it easier to sort them into the right order.

**Keep originals.** Merging creates a new file — your original PDFs are unchanged. Always keep your source files in case you need to reorder or re-merge.

**For very large files:** PDF merging in the browser works well for most documents. For very large files (100MB+), performance depends on your device.
```
